  • Canadian Dividends in Retirement
    • grossed up amount is used to determine your OAS clawback
    • $60k in Canadian eligible dividends = $82,800 in income, putting you into OAS clawback range
    • Canadian Dividends
      • Eligible dividends
        • dividends from Canadians corporations that pay tax on profits before dividends are distributed
        • Taxation on Dividends
          • investors dividend amount is "gross-up" by 138%
          • investors get dividend tax credits
          • marginal tax rate on eligible dividends is usually much lower than marginal tax rate on working income
          • "gross up" and credit used to avoid double taxation on dividends
        • where to hold
          • BEST (non-reg) : to get dividend tax credit
          • OK (RRSP,RRIF,LIRA,TFSA,RESP) : but don't get dividend tax credit
      • NON-eligible dividends
        • usually paid out by REITs and income trusts that distribute dividends BEFORE paying taxes
        • where to hold
          • BEST (RRSP,RRIF,LIRA,TFSA,RESP) : especially if you enroll in DRIP
          • so-so (non-reg) : accounting gets messy if you enroll in DRIP
    • Foreign dividends
      • US dividends
        • withholding tax rate: typically 15% for Canadians (due to tax treaty)
          • exempt when held in retirement account (i.e. RRSP, RRIF)
          • NOT exempt when held in TFSA or RESP (15% withholding tax automatically applied to dividends received)
        • taxed as regular income : foreign tax credits available for use when filing for income tax
        • where to hold
          • BEST (RRSP,RRIF,LIRA) : to avoid 15% withholding tax
          • OK (non-reg): dividends have 15% deducted but can recover through the foreign tax credit.
          • AVOID (TFSA/RESP) : get hit with 15% withholding tax and don't get foreign tax credit
        • NOTE: for limited partnership stocks, REITs and income trusts, you may have to file US-resident income returns
      • non-US dividends
        • withholding tax rate: rate can range from 15-30% (almost every country levies tax on dividends)
        • taxed as regular income : foreign tax credits available for use when filing for income tax
        • where to hold
          • BEST (RRSP,RRIF,LIRA) : to avoid withholding tax IF country has tax treaty with Canada
          • OK (non-reg): dividends have 15% deducted but can recover through the foreign tax credit
          • AVOID (TFSA/RESP) : get hit with 15% withholding tax and don't get foreign tax credit
    • TFSA : good for Canadian dividend paying stocks, ETFs, REITs and income trusts
    • RRSP/LIRA/RRIF : good for US and foreign dividend paying stocks, ETFs, REITs and income trusts
    • non-reg : good for Canadian dividend paying stocks and ETFs that pay eligible dividends
