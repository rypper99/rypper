BONDs

  • 2 pillars of bonds
    • Duration
      • Short (1-5 years) – decrease less when rates rise, increase less when rates drop
      • Mid (5-10 years) – decrease medium when rates rise, increase medium when rates drop
      • Long (10+ years) – decrease most when rates rise, increase most when rates drop
    • Credit of bond (credit rating)
      • Government/Federal – AAA rating (most secure)
      • Provincial
      • Corporate (range from investment grade to high yield)
  • Individual bonds trade over the counter – not on an exchange
  • Bond ETF trades on exchange and holds individual bonds
  • Which Bond ETF to buy:
    • Passive
      • own entire bond market, 1 stop ETF – most diversified, holds all segments
      • ex. ZAG aggregate bond
    • Active (need to have view on where market will go)
      • ETF specific to a pillar of bonds (i.e. short term)
      • Buy ETF based on how you think market will be moving
  • ETF Bond Terms
    • Benchmark info (which index it follows)
    • Inception date (when created)
    • Distribution Frequency (i.e. monthly)
    • Net Assets (how big)
    • Yield to Maturity (rate of return for bond)
      • Will vary based on market and rates – what bond could sell for
    • Annualized Distribution Yield
      • What ETF pays out (minus fee)
    • Coupon (annual interest rate that is % of face value)
      • Always stays the same
    • Duration
      • Indicates how sensitive it is when interest rates rise and fall
  • Management Fee – what provider is making
  • MER – includes Management Fee + tax + trading fees (minimal)
    • ZAG is 0.09% (very low), US bonds more expensive (more work), active fixed income (0.65 or more)
  • Reasons why ETF return less than Index return
    • Index has no MER
    • Index assumes trades occur immediately but fund takes time to make trades and market changes (should be around 10-15 basis points)
  • Holdings page
    • Reviewing specific bond holdings – not too useful for investor (unlike in an equity fund)
    • But check out Holding Allocations
      • Sector (federal, provincial, corporate, municipal)
      • Geographic (Canada)
      • Maturity leads to weighted average duration
      • Credit
    • How to gauge how I am doing with my Bond fund?
      • As rates change
        • Bond price will go up/down
        • Interest receiving with go up/down
