RRIF

Facts

  • Assets can be transferred in kind from RRSP to RRIF
  • RRIF can be opened anytime
  • RRSP must be converted to RRIF by end of your 71st year
  • RRIF must generate income
    • Minimum withdraw percentage based on age
    • Ex. RRIF @ 71 then at 72 will have to take out 5.28% of RRIF value on Jan 1 of 72nd year
  • Successor Annuitant - Leave RRIF to partner to continue to get income when you die
  • Beneficiary - Leave RRIF to child or estate
  • Taxes
    • Don't have to make tax remittance from RRIF account if at minimum withdraw
    • Have to make tax remittance from RRIF if you are above minimum withdraw
  • RRIF has minimum withdrawals based upon percentage
  • If your spouse is younger, when you convert RRSP to RRIF you can elect to use spouses age

Strategy

  • Open at 65 you get pension tax credit
  • Open before 71 to leverage lower tax rates
  • Don't have to change investment strategy in RRIF
    • Will need to consider how it will generate income, Plan what comes out and when
  • Time Line Investing
    • Cash (years 1-3)
    • Income / Balanced (years 4-6)
    • Growth (years 7-10)
      • High quality / diversification with high quality blue chip stocks and ETFs
      • Periodically harvest profits
  • RRIF must generate income
    • If don't need income, can do in-kind transfer to non-reg account
    • You will need to have cash oriented assets ready to pay out when you need income
