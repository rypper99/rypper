RRSP

Facts

  • Can contribute until the end of the year you turn 71
  • On year you turn 71 (by Dec 31) must buy an annuity or convert it into a RRIF
  • Can convert RRSP to RRIF before age 71, when converting:
    • Choose your age or spouses (if younger) to use to determine minimum withdrawal
    • Decide frequency to take withdrawals (monthly, quarterly, annually)
    • Whether you want any withholding tax taken by financial institution
  • No restrictions on withdrawals

Strategy

RRSP vs TFSA

  • Assumptions:
    • Earn $5,000 in top bracket (50% tax) - pay $2,500 in tax
    • RRSP and TFSA invested the same
    • RRSP and TFSA balances will triple over time period
  • RRSP
    • contribute $5,000 to RRSP
      • $2,500 from after tax money
      • $2,500 from tax return - due to $5,000 RRSP contribution
    • initial value of $5,000
    • triples to $15,000
    • withdraw (assume 50% tax rate) = $7,500
  • TFSA
    • contribute $2,500 to TFSA
      • no extra to contribute as no tax deducation for TFSA
    • initial value of $2,000
    • triples to $7,500
    • withdraw (not taxed) = $7,500
  • Summary
    • assuming 50% tax rate - both RRSP and TFSA will yield $7,500
    • what if your tax rate is lower at withdrawal (i.e. 30%)
      • RRSP returns $10,500 (only 30% tax on withdraw)
      • TFSA returns $7,500 (no benfit if withdrawing in lower tax bracket
